What is Wage Garnishment?

Wage garnishment is one of the most direct tools a judgment creditor can use to collect on a debt. It allows a court-ordered portion of a debtor’s paycheck to be withheld and applied toward the judgment.

Discuss Wage Garnishment

Wage garnishment is a legal process that allows a creditor to collect money directly from a debtor’s wages after a judgment has been entered.

Instead of waiting for the debtor to pay voluntarily, the creditor can use the court’s authority to reach a portion of the debtor’s disposable earnings.

How It Works

Once the judgment creditor takes the proper legal steps, the court can issue a garnishment order to the debtor’s employer. The employer then withholds the required amount from the debtor’s paycheck and sends it toward the judgment.

Step 1: Get the writ

The creditor requests the court to issue the garnishment paperwork needed to begin the process.

Step 2: Serve the employer

The employer receives the order and becomes responsible for withholding wages as required by law.

Step 3: Apply payments

The withheld money is forwarded to the creditor or the court and credited against the judgment balance.

Why It Matters

Wage garnishment can create steady payments over time and may be especially useful when a debtor has regular employment but is not paying voluntarily.

It also puts pressure on the debtor to resolve the debt because the process continues until the judgment is paid, the garnishment ends, or another legal outcome changes the case.

What Can Be Garnished

Garnishment law varies by state, but the basic idea is the same: a portion of disposable earnings may be taken to satisfy the judgment.

Regular wages

A portion of the debtor’s paycheck may be withheld from each pay period.

Disposable income

Only income left after required deductions is typically considered.

Ongoing payments

The garnishment can continue until the debt is satisfied or the court stops it.

Important Limits

  • Exemption laws protect certain minimum amounts so the debtor is not left without basic income.
  • Some kinds of income may be exempt or treated differently under state or federal law.
  • Employers must follow the court order carefully, but the exact procedure depends on the jurisdiction.

Wage garnishment is a powerful collection tool because it turns a court judgment into a recurring payment stream. When used correctly, it helps judgment creditors recover money in a structured and enforceable way.
