Judgment Basics

Winning a judgment is only the first step. Many judgment creditors need to use collection tools to locate assets, apply pressure to pay, or force payment through court-approved enforcement methods.

The collection process can involve more than one tool, depending on the debtor’s situation and what information is available.

Why this page exists: this page is built to help judgment creditors understand the major collection methods available after a court win.

Our goal is to give clear next steps and help you decide which method may fit best.

Learn About Collection Techniques

Explore the different ways to collect on a judgment

Debtor’s Examination

Click to learn more

A debtor’s examination is a court process where the debtor answers questions about income, assets, bank accounts, and property. It is used to help the creditor find information that may lead to collection.

Property Liens

Click to learn more

A property lien places a legal claim on real estate owned by the debtor. If the property is sold or refinanced, the lien may have to be paid from the proceeds before the owner can receive the funds. At times creditors can force the sale of assets to satisfy judgments.

Wage Garnishment

Click to learn more

Wage garnishment allows a creditor to collect part of the debtor’s paycheck through an employer after following the required court process and legal limits.

Turnover Orders

Click to learn more

A turnover order directs the debtor or another person to turn over property or funds that can be used to satisfy the judgment. It is particularly useful for real property and intellectual property.

Bank Levies

Click to learn more

A bank levy is a legal process used to seize funds from a debtor’s bank account. It can be effective when the creditor knows where the debtor banks and the account contains collectable funds.

Writ of Execution

Click to learn more

A writ of execution is a court order that authorizes seizure of property to satisfy a judgment. Depending on the situation, a sheriff or other authorized officer may carry out the seizure process.

Subpoenas

Click to learn more

Subpoenas are used to request documents or testimony from a person or business. In judgment collection, they help uncover assets, income, and financial records that support enforcement.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is judgment enforcement?

Judgment enforcement is the process of using legal tools to try to recover money after the courts rule in your favor.

Can you help with unpaid judgments?

Yes. We help with judgment collection and enforcement strategies.

What is a judgment debtor?

A judgment debtor is the person or business that owes money under a court judgment.

Do you work in Washington, Idaho, and Oregon?

Yes. We serve judgment creditors across the Pacific Northwest.
